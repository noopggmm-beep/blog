## 1. ⚖️ DJI vs. Insta360 Patent War Escalates Across China and the US

The biggest IP event of the week. On June 9, Insta360 launched its Luna handheld gimbal camera in the US. The very next day, DJI filed a lawsuit in the US District Court for the Eastern District of Texas, accusing Luna of infringing 2 design patents and 4 invention patents. Insta360 immediately counter-sued in the US over 5 invention patents while simultaneously filing invalidation requests against DJI's corresponding Chinese patents. DJI is seeking a permanent injunction and treble damages.

**Source**: CNR News, Beijing News, Nanfang+

## 2. 📊 The Market Behind the Gimbal Camera Patent War

Global gimbal camera shipments grew over 100% year-on-year in 2025, with DJI holding approximately 62.4% market share and Insta360 about 20.4%. Insta360's 35.5 billion yuan lock-up shares were released on June 11, and the stock has fallen over 30% year-to-date. This litigation is seen as a landmark event in Chinese tech companies' transition from "scale expansion" to "high-quality competition."

**Source**: Eastmoney, The Cover

## 3. 🔗 Tracing the DJI-Insta360 Patent Dispute Origins

In March 2026, DJI had already sued Insta360 in Shenzhen over 6 drone patent ownership disputes, alleging that inventors named in Insta360's patents were former DJI employees. Insta360 founder Liu Jingkang publicly responded: "We respect intellectual property, but we do not fear any patent litigation. We do not engage in stock competition."

**Source**: IPRdaily

## 4. 🇬🇧 UK Bans Social Media for Under-16s — New Data Compliance Challenges

Prime Minister Starmer announced a comprehensive ban on social media use for users under 16, expected to take effect in spring 2027. The ban will have far-reaching implications for platforms' data collection, user privacy protection, and IP compliance.

**Source**: BBC

## 5. 🇫🇷🇺🇸 US-France Digital Services Tax Triggers Trade Friction — IP Tariff Threats

During the G7 summit, Trump threatened 100% tariffs on French wine in retaliation for France's digital services tax. The digital tax involves cross-border pricing and profit allocation of tech companies' intellectual property, making it a major dispute at the intersection of international taxation and IP.

**Source**: Reuters

## 6. 🧬 China's High-Purity Silicon-28 Mass Production — Strategic Core Material IP

CNNC achieved 99.99% purity silicon-28 independent mass production, the core material for silicon-based quantum chips. The indigenous IP portfolio surrounding this technological breakthrough will be a critical safeguard for the development of China's quantum computing industry.

**Source**: Xinhua News Agency, CCTV News

## 7. 🏛️ G7 Summit Focuses on Emerging Technology Governance — Global AI IP Framework

Core G7 agenda items included "emerging technology governance," covering cutting-edge IP issues such as copyright ownership of AI-generated content and global licensing of standard-essential patents (SEPs). Disagreements among nations on AI IP protection rules were a key focus.

**Source**: NHK WORLD-JAPAN

## 8. 🚀 SpaceX IPO — Revaluing Space Technology IP

SpaceX went public at a valuation of $1.77 trillion. Its core assets include technology patent portfolios covering rocket recovery and Starlink communications. The capital market pricing of IP value in the space technology sector drew significant attention.

**Source**: CNBC

## 9. 📱 AI World Foundation Model Released — Training Data IP Disputes on the Horizon

BAAI released the "Wujie·Physis-v0.1" general world foundation model. As AI model capabilities leap forward, the IP provenance and compliant use of training data have become focal points for industry and legal communities.

**Source**: Beijing Daily

## 10. 🔬 Quantum Spin Liquid Experimental Breakthrough — Fundamental Science Patent Race Accelerates

University College Cork achieved the first experimental evidence of "spinons." Global patent applications in the field of topological quantum computing have grown rapidly in recent years, and fundamental scientific breakthroughs are accelerating the patent race.

**Source**: Xinhua News Agency
