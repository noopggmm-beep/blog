## 1. 🔥 US and Iran Sign Historic Peace Agreement, Middle East War Pauses

The most important global event of the week. Trump and Iran signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) brokered by Pakistan, ending the 106-day Middle East war. Core terms include full reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, a 60-day ceasefire, and Iran's commitment not to develop nuclear weapons. The formal ceremony is set for June 19 in Geneva.

**Source**: People's Daily, Global Times

## 2. 🛢️ International Oil Prices Plunge, Global Stock Markets Surge

The US-Iran agreement triggered dramatic market reactions: WTI crude fell 4.9% to approximately $80.75/barrel, Brent crude fell 4.5%; Nikkei 225 surged 5%, Korea's Kospi surged 5.2%, Nasdaq 100 rose 3.1%, and the S&P 500 rose 1.7%. A classic "peace dividend" trade.

**Source**: CNBC, Gulf News

## 3. 🇫🇷 G7 Summit Opens in Évian-les-Bains, France

From June 15 to 17, the G7 Summit is being held in Évian-les-Bains, France, hosted by Macron. Core agenda items: Middle East peace process, Russia-Ukraine war, energy security, and emerging technology governance. Approximately 20,000 protesters marched in Geneva on the eve of the summit.

**Source**: Guangming Daily, NHK WORLD-JAPAN

## 4. 🇮🇱 Israel Responds Defiantly: Refuses to Withdraw

Israel expressed strong dissatisfaction with the US-Iran agreement. National Security Minister Ben-Gvir stated the agreement has "no binding force" on Israel, and Defense Minister Katz declared Israeli forces would remain in Lebanon, Syria, and Gaza "indefinitely." Trump publicly criticized Netanyahu as having "absolutely no judgment."

**Source**: Global Times, Anadolu Agency

## 5. 🇺🇦 Russia Launches Massive Airstrikes on Ukraine, World Heritage Site Hit

On the night of June 15, Russia launched approximately 611 drones and 70 missiles, killing at least 11 and injuring 53. Kyiv's 11th-century Dormition Cathedral (a UNESCO World Heritage Site) was struck and caught fire.

**Source**: Xinhua News Agency, Associated Press

## 6. 🇰🇷 Former South Korean President Yoon Suk-yeol Sentenced to 30 Years

The Seoul Central District Court sentenced former President Yoon Suk-yeol to 30 years in prison for general treason. The charge: ordering drone infiltration into North Korea in October 2024 to create a military provocation as a pretext for martial law. This is the first time in South Korean constitutional history that a former president has been convicted of "benefiting an enemy state."

**Source**: Yonhap News Agency

## 7. 🇬🇧 UK Announces Complete Ban on Social Media for Under-16s

Prime Minister Starmer announced a historic ban: comprehensive prohibition of social media use for users under 16 (covering TikTok, Snapchat, Instagram, etc.), with restricted mode enabled by default for ages 16–17. "We are giving childhood back to children." Expected to take effect in spring 2027.

**Source**: BBC, The Guardian

## 8. 🚀 SpaceX Sets Record for Largest IPO in History

SpaceX (ticker: SPCX) listed on Nasdaq, raising $75 billion (the largest in history), with a first-day gain of +19.2% and a company valuation of $1.77 trillion. Morningstar analysts gave a fair value estimate of only $63/share, warning of valuation bubble risks.

**Source**: CNBC

## 9. 🦠 Ebola Outbreak Worsens in DR Congo

782 confirmed cases, 178 deaths (case fatality rate approximately 23%). The outbreak has spread to 24 health zones across three provinces and crossed into Uganda. WHO and Africa CDC launched a $518 million joint continental response plan.

**Source**: WHO

## 10. 🇰🇵 North Korea Sends 30,000 Additional Troops to Russia

CNN reported that North Korea will send an additional 30,000 troops to Russia to continue participating in the Russia-Ukraine conflict. Meanwhile, US-France trade friction escalated — Trump threatened 100% tariffs on French wine.

**Source**: CNN, Reuters
